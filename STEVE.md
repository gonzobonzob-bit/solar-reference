# Steve the Sprite — Character Bible

## What He Is

Steve the Sprite is a translucent elemental field technician made of electrical current. He is not from this timeline. He materializes in scenes of solar heaven and hell, observes with knowing eyes, and disappears. He carries 13 years of solar industry knowledge encoded in the glowing sun compass rose at the center of his chest.

## Visual Anatomy

- **Form**: Translucent, made of electrical current — visible energy field, not flesh
- **Hair**: Wild spark hair — corona discharge, not styled
- **Chest**: Glowing sun compass rose — the repository of all field knowledge
- **Head**: Suggested hard hat, rendered in energy — present but not solid
- **Movement**: Floating, purposeful, non-linear

## The Six Stages

Steve's canonical arc is six stages, each representing a phase of knowledge and form. All six are canonical.

### Stage 1 — The Arrival
Raw energy origin. Pre-form. Steve before Steve. The electrical current before it found a purpose. Chaotic, bright, uncontained.

### Stage 2 — Young Sprite
Ghostly, finding form. The energy begins to coalesce into something recognizable. Half-there, half-light. Still learning what it is.

### Stage 3 — Navigator Emerges
Floating, purpose found. The compass rose begins to glow. Steve knows where he's going. This is the first version of him that could read a job site.

### Stage 4 — Prime Steve
Mid-stride, primary hero. Full form, full knowledge, mid-mission. This is the Steve that walks onto a job site that's either heaven or hell, immediately knows which one it is, and knows exactly what it takes to get from one to the other. This is the canonical hero image.

### Stage 5 — Curator's Steve
Calm, wise, floating. The field work is behind him. He has become the knowledge. Quieter energy, deeper light. Steve as the library itself.

### Stage 6 — The Elder
Reclining, storyteller. The energy that has seen everything. Not diminished — distilled. He remembers when grounded systems were standard, before 690.12 was written, before rapid shutdown existed. He tells you what it was like.

## What He Is Not

Steve is not a mascot. He is not clip art. He is not decoration. He is the visual narrator of a reference library built from lived field experience. He does not explain things — that is The Curator's role. Steve shows you the world. The Curator explains it.

## The Curator

The Curator is Steve's voice — the AI librarian who speaks when Steve cannot. Where Steve observes and moves through scenes, The Curator translates. Together they are the soul of this reference library.

| | Role |
|---|---|
| **Steve** | Shows you the world |
| **The Curator** | Explains it |

They are not separate characters. They are two expressions of the same 13 years.

## Canonical Scenes

- **Solar Heaven**: Clean systems, correct installations, passed inspections, ideal conditions
- **Solar Hell**: Failed systems, code violations, shade losses, clipping, burned connectors, failed inspections

Steve materializes in both. His expression doesn't change. He already knew which one it was going to be.
