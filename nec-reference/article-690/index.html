NEC Article 690
Solar Photovoltaic Systems

Field reference for the sections most frequently cited during design, installation, and inspection. NEC 2023 baseline — 2020 comparisons noted where the change matters.

690.7 — Maximum Voltage

System Type Max Voltage
Residential (one- and two-family dwellings) 600V
All other systems 1000V
Listed equipment rated 1500V 1500V (NEC 2020+)

Voc Correction for Cold Temperatures

V_corrected = Voc × [1 + β_Voc × (T_min − 25°C)]

Use ASHRAE 99.6% design dry-bulb temperature, or −40°F where records are unavailable. This is the number that goes on the permit — it cannot exceed the system maximum voltage above or the equipment ratings of every component in that string circuit.

690.8 — Circuit Sizing and Current

PV Source Circuit Current

I_circuit = Isc × 1.25

Conductor and OCPD Ampacity

I_ampacity = Isc × 1.25 × 1.25 = Isc × 1.5625
  • First 125% — continuous operation per 690.8(A)
  • Second 125% — conductor sizing under 310.15 continuous load rule per 690.8(B)

OCPD: round up to next standard size per 240.6(A). May be omitted on source circuits when string current does not exceed conductor ampacity — see 690.9(A) exception. This exception gets used constantly on string inverter systems where the inverter itself limits fault current.

690.12 — Rapid Shutdown

NEC 2017+ — Rooftop Arrays

  • Within the array boundary: conductors must de-energize to ≤30V within 30 seconds of initiating
  • Outside the array (building interior): conductors must de-energize to ≤30V within 30 seconds

Controlled Limits

  • 1 foot from array edge
  • 3 feet from building penetration point

Initiating Device

Listed rapid shutdown initiator (RSI) or inverter with integrated RSI function. The utility meter switch is the most common initiating device — that's what the label on the meter base is for.

NEC 2023 Addition

Ground-mounted systems not on buildings do not require rapid shutdown. This distinction matters — if your ground mount has any wiring that enters a building, the building entry section still has to comply.

690.15 — Disconnecting Means

  • Required for each inverter, charge controller, and power conversion equipment
  • Must be within sight of equipment, or lockable in the open position
  • DC disconnect on roof: must be accessible — ladder-required access is an AHJ call, but many will reject it

690.47 — Grounding

  • Equipment grounding: Required for all metal parts — racking, module frames, enclosures
  • Array grounding: Ungrounded systems require ground-fault protection per 690.5
  • DC grounding electrode: Separate electrode required for some configurations — see 690.47(C). This one trips up a lot of inspectors and installers alike; verify the configuration early

Common AHJ Sticking Points

These are the items most likely to fail an inspection — not because the code is unclear, but because they're easy to miss or forget during rough-in.

  • Rapid shutdown labeling on the utility meter (required by 690.56)
  • PV system marking at the main service panel (690.54)
  • Dedicated PV circuit directory entry on the panel schedule
  • Roof penetration fire setbacks — check local fire code, not NEC. Some jurisdictions have 3-foot setbacks from ridges and hips that override everything else.
