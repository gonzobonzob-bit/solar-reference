NEC Article 705
Interconnected Electric Power Production Sources

Covers how solar PV — and other on-site generation — connects back to the utility system. This is the article that governs everything from the inverter output to the point where it touches the utility grid. NEC 2023 baseline.

705.12 — Point of Connection

Three main interconnection methods. Most residential jobs use load-side. Know all three because the AHJ, utility, or system size will sometimes force one over the others.

Supply-Side Connection — 705.12(A)

  • Connect on the utility side of the main service disconnect
  • No 120% bus bar rule limitation — the panel rating is not the constraint here
  • Requires utility approval and often a second meter
  • Common for larger commercial systems where load-side would require a panel upgrade

Load-Side Connection — 705.12(B)

The most common residential method. The 120% rule is what makes it work.

120% Bus Bar Rule

Max PV OCPD = (Bus bar rating × 120%) − Main breaker rating

Example: 200A panel, 200A main breaker

Max PV breaker = (200 × 1.2) − 200 = 40A
  • PV breaker must be at the opposite end of the bus from the main breaker
  • "Opposite end" means physically opposite in the panel — not just labeled differently, not just on a different row. Physically at the other end of the bus bar.
  • This gets failed on inspection more than anything else in 705

Dedicated Fusible Disconnect — 705.12(D)

  • Tap conductors connected to service entrance conductors
  • Limited to specific configurations; less common in residential
  • Requires utility coordination in most jurisdictions

705.20 — Disconnecting Means

  • Required for each source of power
  • Must be accessible and indicate open/closed position
  • For interactive inverters: the inverter's integrated disconnect typically satisfies this — confirm with the listing and your AHJ

Backfeed Labeling — 705.12(B)(3)

Breakers used for backfeed must carry a permanent label:

WARNING — DUAL POWER SOURCES — DO NOT RELOCATE THIS BREAKER

Some AHJs require a matching caution label on the panel door as well — ask before you close up. Forgetting this label will fail the final inspection every time, and it cannot be added after the panel cover is on without re-opening.
