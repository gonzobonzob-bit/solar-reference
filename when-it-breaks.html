Diagnose the Problem

The system looked fine. Until it didn't. This section is the diagnostic side of solar — the part no one pitches you during the sales call. What fails, why it fails, what it costs, and how to tell the difference between a monitoring glitch and a real problem.

By the Numbers

Solar underperformance is not rare, and the causes are not random. The data points to consistent failure patterns — and consistent blind spots in how systems are sold and installed.

39%
Of power losses in solar arrays are caused by inverter failures — the single largest category ahead of string and combiner issues.
$10B
In global solar underperformance losses in 2024. Per-MW losses grew from $977 in 2020 to $5,720 in 2024 — a nearly 6× increase in four years.
60%
Maximum power loss documented during extreme weather events. Short-term but increasingly common as grid stress events intensify.

Failure Mode Breakdown

Inverters lead at 39%, but the full picture across hardware categories shows no single point is safe from failure:

Failure Category Share of Power Losses Notes
Inverters 39% Communication faults, MPPT drift, thermal shutdown, hardware failure
Strings 22% Open circuits, shade-induced mismatch, bypass diode failure, degraded modules
Combiners 19% Fuse failures, loose terminations, moisture ingress in poorly sealed enclosures
Other / Environmental 20% Soiling, shading, weather events, monitoring gaps
