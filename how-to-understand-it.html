The Reference Stack

Seven categories of source material. This is the foundation everything else in the library is built on — the documents, standards, and publications that define what solar is supposed to be, and how we measure the distance between that and what it actually is.

Code Foundation
NEC Articles

690 (PV Systems), 705 (Interconnected Power Production Sources), 706 (Energy Storage Systems), 480 (Battery Systems), 250 (Grounding), 300 (Wiring Methods). The baseline. Cross-referenced throughout the library with article and section precision.

Building
Industry Publications
Trade Press & Research

PV Magazine, Solar Power World, Wood Mackenzie Solar, Raptor Maps industry reports. Current data on system performance, failure rates, industry trends, and underperformance economics. Citations linked at source.

Building
Authority Having Jurisdiction
AHJ References

Every jurisdiction interprets the NEC differently. What to expect from local amendments, how to document pre-inspection conversations, what common local deviations look like in the field. Closely tied to The Unapproved Files.

→ See: AHJ Encounters
Equipment Standards
Manufacturer Standards

UL listings, IEC standards, equipment installation manuals. The documents that live between the NEC and the actual equipment on the roof. Warranty terms, compatibility requirements, torque specs, temperature ratings.

Building
Credential Standard
NABCEP

North American Board of Certified Energy Practitioners. The credential benchmark for installation and design professionals. Study resources, exam domains, and what the credential actually represents versus what it's sometimes sold as.

Building
Grid Connection
Utility Interconnection

Net metering tariffs, interconnection agreements, application processes, technical screens, and the paperwork that sits between a working solar system and the grid. The part most installers hand off and most homeowners never see.

Building
Structural Code Crossover
IBC / IRC Crossover

Where solar intersects with structural code. Wind load calculations, snow load requirements, racking attachment methods, penetration sealing, and the often-overlooked connection between the electrical permit and the building permit.

Building
Field Intelligence
The Unapproved Files

Where the reference stack meets the job site. What the documents say versus what the field finds. AHJ encounters, unapproved observations, and case files from 13 years of being in rooms where solar either went right or very visibly did not.

From the Field

The Questions Nobody Asks

After 13 years across the full solar lifecycle, the same knowledge gaps come up again and again — from homeowners who were sold a system without being taught how to read it, and from installers who were trained on code without being taught how to explain it. This section exists to close those gaps.

The reference stack above is the foundation. The modules being built from it are the explanation layer — what these documents actually mean when they hit the real world, and what the real world reveals about them when they don't. Check back as this section develops.

Disclaimer: Content on this site includes cited code references from NEC or official sources, and personal opinion from 13 years of solar industry experience. Personal opinion is clearly labeled as such. Human error is possible — if you spot something incorrect or outdated, we want to know. Nothing on this site constitutes legal, engineering, or compliance advice. Always verify requirements with your local Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ).
