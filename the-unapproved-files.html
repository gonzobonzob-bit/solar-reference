Case File #001
"The Workhorse and the Two Complainers"
Commercial Ground Mount String Inverters Rural Utility Feed Repeat RMA

Three string inverters, commercial ground mount, rural utility feed. Two units on repeat RMA, one never failing.

Root Cause: Industrial water treatment plant on the same rural feeder causing voltage and frequency fluctuations. The two failing units were catching it — the third was sitting in a section of the feeder that happened to be buffered enough to ride through it.

Fix: Ferrite cores and common-mode chokes on AC output lines.

Filed By: Gonzo — Special Investigator, Field Operations
Curator's Cross-Reference: NEC 690.5  ·  IEEE 1547  ·  UL 1741
Case File #002
"The Diode Dance"
Residential Rooftop Micro Inverter System Bypass Diode Activation Shade Analysis

Residential rooftop, micro inverter system. Installer blamed hardware failure.

Root Cause: Tree growth over time shading the array during peak hours. Bypass diode activation dropping string from 36V nominal to 24V mid-day with one diode active. Monitoring showed a flat line with small production bumps first and last hour only.

Fix: Tree assessment, shade analysis, possible array redesign.

Filed By: Gonzo — Special Investigator, Field Operations
Curator's Cross-Reference: NEC 690.4  ·  IEC 61730  ·  IEEE 1562

The Record

Why This Section Exists

Solar installations are documented for permits, not for posterity. The paperwork captures what passed, not what was found. The photos show the finished job, not the condition it was in when the crew arrived. The final inspection record says approved — it doesn't note the three things that were fixed the morning of the inspection.

This section is the difference between what got approved and what actually happened. It is not a collection of complaints. It is a collection of evidence — from 13 years of being present on job sites where the distance between what was designed and what was built was visible, measurable, and in some cases significant.

Nothing here is fabricated. The names don't matter. The patterns do.
